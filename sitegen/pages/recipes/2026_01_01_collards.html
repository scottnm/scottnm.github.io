Happy New Years! These collards were a hit this year, so I wanted to immortalize them in my internet junk drawer

the recipe

Ingredients

Equipment

  • 1 large stock pot
  • 1 colander
  • 1 large mixing bowl

Food

  • 2 bunches of collards
  • 4 strips of bacon
  • 1 tbsp extra bacon fat (optional)
  • 1 bouillon cube
  • 1 white onion
  • 6 cloves of garlic

Instructions

Prep work

  • Separate greens from stems and wash dirt of a greens by soaking in a bowl of running water and then dumping through colander
  • layer all the collard leaves together, roll them like a cigar, and cut them into ½ inch strips
  • Dice the onion; set aside
  • Mince the garlic; set aside

Cooking

  • Cook 4 strips of bacon like 75% of the way through in a large stock pot rendering out the fat (should only take a handful of minutes).
  • Remove bacon from stockpot leaving rendered out bacon fat behind
  • (Optional) if you really want to pack a punch, add the extra, optional tbsp of bacon fat
  • Add minced garlic cloves and let cook but not fry in bacon fat for a few minutes
  • Add two cups of water to stock pot to deglaze the pot
  • Stir and scrap to get any good bits off the bottom of the pot
  • Add bouillion cube and stir until dissolved
  • Cut up the removed bacon into ½ inch pieces
  • Add the collards and cut bacon to pot
  • Mix well to incorporate collards, bacon, and broth together
  • Press collards mixture down into pot a bit if they're sitting too high above the broth mixture
  • Top with just enough water so that if you press down on the collards, you can see the water just below
  • Cover pot and let simmer or 1.5 to 2 hours
  • Stir occassionally to ensure collards cook and wilt evenly
  • Check on broth throughout and adjust the heat so that the broth reduces to a good potlikker
  • Once time's up, season to taste with salt and pepper
